# MoneyShow Sponsor/Exhibitor ICP

**Campaign:** B2B Sponsor & Exhibitor Outreach
**Last updated:** 2026-05-27
**Owner:** Michael Berger, CMO

---

## Target Firm Types

**Primary verticals:**
1. Asset managers / RIAs (mutual funds, ETF issuers, hedge funds, registered investment advisors)
2. Brokers / trading platforms (retail brokers, futures/options platforms)
3. Fintech / tools (portfolio analytics, research platforms, charting tools)
4. Newsletter / media publishers (paid financial newsletters, research services, advisory letters)

**Excluded verticals:**
- B2C consumer brands outside finance (no audience fit)
- Crypto-only / highly speculative platforms (brand risk — evaluate case by case)
- Sub-$100M AUM boutiques with no identifiable marketing budget

---

## Company Size Floor

- **AUM:** $100M+
- **Employees:** 10+
- Sweet spot: established, growth-stage financial firms with a dedicated marketing or distribution function

---

## Target Titles

**Primary (high intent — owns budget):**
- CMO / VP Marketing
- Head of Advisor Relations / Director of Distribution
- CEO / Founder (at firms <25 employees where the founder controls marketing spend)

**Secondary (influence, not budget):**
- Director of Events / Partnerships (at larger firms — confirm they have budget authority)
- Head of Retail / Wholesale Distribution

**Never target:**
- Coordinators, analysts, assistants
- Compliance-only contacts
- Contacts without a verified email

---

## What Sponsors Are Buying

**Primary value proposition:** Brand awareness with MoneyShow's audience of affluent retail investors and active traders — one of the most concentrated, high-net-worth investor audiences in the U.S.

**Supporting value points:**
- 45+ years of audience trust and brand credibility
- Live event + virtual reach (both in-person and digital exposure)
- Direct access to investors actively looking for products and strategies to deploy capital

**Offer framing for outreach:** Lead with audience quality and reach, not logistics. The sponsor is buying exposure to serious investors — not just a booth or a logo placement.

---

## Buying Signals (prioritize these in Apollo/enrichment)

- Recently launched a new retail product, fund, or platform
- Hiring roles in marketing, distribution, or investor relations (signals growth budget)
- Active spend on financial media ads (Barron's, Investopedia, Bloomberg)
- Speaking at or sponsoring other investor conferences (confirms category budget exists)
- New leadership in marketing or distribution (new exec = new budget review)

---

## Anti-ICP

| Signal | Reason |
|---|---|
| B2C consumer brand (non-finance) | Audience mismatch |
| No identifiable marketing budget or contact | Below size floor |
| Contact missing verified email or first name | Breaks personalization |
| Currently on opt-out list | Do not contact |

---

## Known Objections

1. "Our audience is advisors / institutions, not retail investors" → Counter: MoneyShow's retail audience includes self-directed HNW investors who mirror advisor-managed portfolios; many are also RIAs themselves.
2. "We already sponsor [other conference]" → Counter: not exclusive; most financial sponsors run 3–6 event placements per year.
3. "We need to see the attendee demographics first" → Counter: have the attendee profile deck ready as proof point.
